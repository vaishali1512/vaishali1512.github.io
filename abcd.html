A Supply chain is a network involving a series of steps to make a product or service reach from the manufacturers to the buyers. The management of all the processes ranging from planning, controlling, and distribution to verifying the flow of a product or service in the most secure and cost effective manner is what we call supply chain management. With the advent of complex production and distribution mechanisms, the transition between different phases of product required a lot of manual work and was prone to a lot of human error. Auditing and logging services required a lot of paper drill in traditional linear supply chain systems.[4] There is a need of more dynamic, demand-driven environment and a customer-focused marketplace. Cloud services have transformed conventional chains into cost-effective and reduced risk of manual errors. But major problems like authentication, real-time tracking and resolving the disputes still remains. There is need of system which is verifiable, secure, low operational cost, scalable and efficiently manages logistics.
